FAQs

Start by reviewing your spending habits and financial goals.

  • If you pay in full each month, focus on cards with high rewards, travel points, or cashback
  • If you carry a balance, prioritize low interest rate cards or 0% balance transfer promotions.
  • If you’re building credit, look for no-fee or student credit cards with simple approval criteria.

Most major banks offer instant online approval if you meet their criteria. In some cases, further review is needed, which may take a few business days. Once approved, expect your card to arrive by mail in 5–10 business days.

Yes — many Canadians carry two or more cards. Having multiple cards can:

  • Increase your overall credit limit (helping your credit utilization ratio).
  • Give you access to different rewards categories or perks.

But be cautious: too many cards can make tracking balances harder, which increases the risk of missed payments.

If you miss a payment:

  • You’ll be charged a late payment fee (typically $10–$30).
  • Your interest rate may rise (some cards apply penalty rates above 25%).
  • Your missed payment may be reported to credit bureaus, hurting your credit score.

If you’re having trouble making payments, contact your card issuer — many offer hardship programs or payment plans.

A balance transfer allows you to move debt from one credit card to another — usually at a promotional low interest rate (sometimes as low as 0% for 6–12 months). This can save you money on interest and help you pay off debt faster.

However, always check:

  • Balance transfer fees (typically 1–3%).
  • How long the promo rate lasts.
  • What the rate increases to after the promo ends.

A balance transfer is most effective if you can pay off the balance before the promo period ends.

Most Canadian credit cards charge a foreign transaction fee of around 2.5–3% on purchases made in a non-Canadian currency.

To avoid these fees, choose a card with no foreign transaction fees — great for online shopping in USD or international travel. Aventori highlights these cards in our comparisons.

Using a credit card responsibly can improve your credit score.

  • Positive impact: Making on-time payments and keeping your balance below 30% of your limit.
  • Negative impact: Missing payments, maxing out your card, or applying for too many new cards in a short period.

Over time, consistent, responsible use builds a strong credit profile.

Unsecured credit cards (most cards on Aventori) don’t require a deposit — approval is based on your credit history.

Secured credit cards require a cash deposit as collateral and are designed for people building or rebuilding credit.

Many Canadian cards offer:

  • Extended warranties (often doubling the manufacturer’s warranty).
  • Purchase protection against theft or damage (usually within 90 days).
  • Travel insurance, including trip interruption/cancellation, rental car coverage, and emergency medical insurance (on premium cards).
