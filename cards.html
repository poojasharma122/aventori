Cards

Choosing the right credit card can be confusing — with so many options, rates, and rewards programs available in Canada, it’s hard to know which one offers the best value. At Aventori, we cut through the noise by reviewing Canada’s top credit cards in detail, including their features, rewards, fees, and who they’re best suited for. Whether you want to save on interest, earn cashback, or rack up travel points, our expert reviews will help you make an informed decision and get the most out of every dollar you spend.

Scotiabank Scene+ Visa Card

Key Features
  • No annual fee.
  • Earn Scene+ points: 2× points on eligible purchases at partner merchants like Sobeys, fresh-foods, Home Hardware, Cineplex etc. 1× point on most other purchases.
  • Redemption options are flexible: points can be used for movie tickets, groceries, dining, travel, shopping, gift cards.
Pros
  • Great for people who shop often at Scene+ partner merchants or go to Cineplex/theatres. You accumulate rewards faster in those categories.
  • Zero annual fee means the card has low cost to own. You don’t have to worry about paying to keep it open.
  • Good entry-level or “starter” rewards card: useful for students, new immigrants, or anyone building credit.
Cons
  • Limited premium perks: fewer travel or insurance benefits compared to high-end cards.
  • If you don’t spend much with Scene+ partners, the rewards are less compelling — the 1× base earning on many purchases makes accumulation slower.
  • Interest rate & foreign transaction fees may be high, so carrying balances or doing lots of travel can reduce value.

Best For

  • People who frequently shop at grocery stores, hardware stores, or movie-theatres that are Scene+ partners.
  • Those looking for a no-fee card with rewards and flexible redemption.
  • Users who pay off their balance monthly, so they avoid interest dragging down the rewards.

Tangerine Money-Back Mastercard

Key Features
  • No annual fee.
  • Customizable cash-back categories: you choose up to 2 categories (grocery, gas, restaurants, furniture, etc.) that earn 2% cash back; a third category becomes available if you deposit your cashback into a Tangerine savings account.
  • Everything else earns 0.5% cash back.
  • Some promos: e.g. 10% extra cash back on up to $1,000 in purchases in your first 2 months. Also a promotional low balance-transfer rate (≈1.95%) for a few months.
Pros
  • Very flexible rewards: being able to pick which categories you want to get bonus cash back in means you can tailor it to your spending habits.
  • No yearly cost and unlimited cash back makes it low risk. Even if you don’t maximize bonus categories, you still get something.
  • Good promos for new cardholders (welcome cash back, balance transfer deals) can add value.
Cons
  • The base rate of 0.5% on non-bonus categories is quite modest. If a lot of your spending falls into categories that are not bonus ones, your effective rewards drop.
  • Lacks many premium benefits (travel insurance, lounge access, etc.) compared with high-annual-fee cards. If you want those perks, you may need a different card.
  • Some of the balance transfer / promo terms may have fees or limitations, so if you’re chasing those offers, you need to read the fine print.

Best For

  • People who want cash back rather than points, and who like to control which categories they get higher return on.
  • Users who can take advantage of the bonus category rewards and often spend in those categories.
  • Anyone who wants a low-maintenance, no-fee card that does good basic rewards.

RBC Avion Visa Infinite

Key Features
  • Earn 1 Avion point per $1 spent on everyday purchases, and extra points on eligible travel bookings through RBC Rewards.
  • Flexible redemption: points can be used for flights on almost any airline with no blackout dates, plus merchandise, gift cards, and statement credits.
  • Comes with travel insurance (trip interruption, rental car coverage, and more).
Pros
  • Extremely flexible rewards program for frequent travelers.
  • Competitive welcome bonus (often 35,000+ points).
  • Includes solid travel insurance benefits.
Cons
  • $120 annual fee (sometimes waived for the first year with promotions).
  • Interest rate of 19.99% (standard for premium rewards cards) — not ideal for carrying a balance.

Best For

  • Frequent travelers who value flexible redemption options and want premium insurance coverage included with their card.

American Express Cobalt Card

Key Features
  • Earn 5x Membership Rewards points on groceries, dining, food delivery, and streaming services.
  • Earn 2x points on travel, transit, and gas, and 1x points on everything else.
  • Flexible points: redeem for travel, statement credits, or transfer to airline/hotel partners (Aeroplan, Marriott Bonvoy).
  • Monthly fee: $12.99 ($155.88 per year).
Pros
  • Market-leading earn rate for dining and grocery purchases.
  • Points can be transferred to major loyalty programs for added value.
  • Great for people who spend heavily on food, entertainment, or streaming.
Cons
  • Monthly fee adds up, so it’s only worth it if you maximize the rewards.
  • American Express isn’t accepted everywhere (though coverage in Canada is strong).

Best For

  • Foodies, travelers, and anyone looking for maximum rewards on daily spending categories.

BMO CashBack Mastercard

Key Features
  • 5% cash back on all purchases for the first 3 months (up to $2,500 spend).
  • After welcome period: 3% cash back on groceries, 1% on recurring bills, and 0.5% on everything else.
  • No annual fee.
Pros
  • Excellent welcome bonus with very high first-3-month cash back.
  • No cost to keep long-term — great as a secondary card.
  • Good option for people who spend a lot on groceries.
Cons
  • Lower base earn rate (0.5%) compared to other cards.
  • No premium perks like insurance or extended warranties.

Best For

  • People who want a no-fee cash back card with strong rewards on groceries and recurring payments.
